**TENANT TARGETING 2.0**

The Base — Schiphol Business District

*Presentation Outline*

### Slide 1: Title Slide

**Objective:** Set expectations. Signal professionalism and a tailored approach.

**Core message:** This is not a standard marketing plan. It is a structured methodology for finding the right companies, at the right time.

**Suggested visual:** *Full-bleed aerial photograph of The Base. Title overlay in dark navy with white text.*

### Slide 2: Why Waiting Doesn't Work

**Objective:** Create the problem that the strategy solves.

**Core message:** The best tenants for The Base are not sitting in a portal search waiting to be found. They need to be identified and approached.

* Most companies begin their office search 12–24 months before lease expiry — quietly, and online
* By the time a company formally enters the market, they typically have 2–3 shortlisted locations already
* Portal listings and passive marketing reach a broad audience but create few qualified conversations
* The Base’s strengths — community, accessibility, ecosystem — are not well communicated through a listing

**Suggested visual:** *Simple funnel diagram contrasting Reactive Leasing vs Intent-Led Leasing.*

### Slide 3: A Methodology, Not a Campaign

**Objective:** Frame Tenant Targeting 2.0 as a structured, professional approach.

**Core message:** Intent-Led Leasing is a five-step process that identifies, maps, approaches, and engages the right companies at the right moment.

* Built on real business signals — not guesswork
* Targets the decision-makers who will actually influence the leasing outcome
* Operates with a consultative, not transactional, tone
* Produces qualified conversations, not unqualified enquiries

**Suggested visual:** *A clean five-step horizontal flow diagram: 1. Detect → 2. Map → 3. Connect → 4. Engage → 5. Convert*

### Slide 4: Why This Suits The Base

**Objective:** Connect the methodology directly to The Base’s specific strengths.

**Core message:** The Base is not a difficult building to sell. It is a difficult story to reach the right audience at the right moment. This method solves that.

* Accessibility by road (A4, A5, A9, A10), rail (8 min to NS station), air (8 min to Schiphol Plaza) — powerful for international teams
* Workplace ecosystem (gym, restaurant, childcare, meeting centre, community) — directly addresses HR and talent retention
* 44,000 m² across four towers — flexibility for companies at different growth stages
* BREEAM Very Good / A+ energy label — meets ESG requirements without additional tenant effort

**Suggested visual:** *Four quadrant graphic — Accessibility, Amenities, Community, and Sustainability.*

### Slide 5: Who We Are Looking For

**Objective:** Show the client that the targeting is specific and commercially credible.

**Core message:** The ideal tenant is a 50–200 person company in a mobile sector, with a likely business trigger that creates an office need.

* Size: 50–200 employees (matching available floor sizes from ~50m² to 2,235m² per floor)
* Sectors: Finance, IT, Aerospace, Cargo/Logistics, Media, Energy
* Geography: A4 corridor (The Hague, Rotterdam, Amsterdam), international companies seeking a Netherlands HQ
* Business triggers: Growth, lease expiry, accessibility issues, desire for better amenities, team consolidation

**Suggested visual:** *Two-column layout — Company Profile characteristics vs Business Triggers with icons.*

### Slide 6: Step 1 — Finding Companies That Are Already Looking

**Objective:** Explain the intent-detection step without technical jargon.

**Core message:** We use tools like DealFront and LeadInfo to identify companies that are actively researching offices — before they contact a broker.

* Companies visiting The Base website, competitor buildings, or office search platforms are surfaced automatically
* Each visitor is matched to a company profile: sector, size, location, growth signals
* Additional signals: LinkedIn headcount growth, funding announcements, public lease expiry data
* Output: a prioritised target list, ranked by relevance and recency of signal

**Suggested visual:** *A simplified data flow graphic: Signal Source → Company Identified → Trigger Assessed → Priority Ranking.*

### Slide 7: Step 2 — Reaching the Whole Decision-Making Unit

**Objective:** Explain why DMU mapping increases conversion and reduces wasted effort.

**Core message:** An office decision involves multiple stakeholders. We identify and approach 5–6 people per target company, each with a tailored message.

* CEO/MD: strategic fit, address quality, neighbour companies (Heineken, Microsoft, Samsung)
* CFO: all-in cost clarity — from €280/m² rent + €65/m² service charge, transparent pricing
* HR Director: gym, childcare, restaurant, community — retention and talent attraction assets
* Operations/Facilities: parking (1:70 ratio, EV charging), lifts, hospitality desk, delivery level
* Workplace Manager: unit flexibility from 50m², flex workspaces via Regus, atrium and collaboration spaces

**Suggested visual:** *Org chart / stakeholder map showing the 5–6 DMU roles with a ‘what matters to them’ tag.*

### Slide 8: Steps 3 & 4 — Starting the Right Conversation, in the Right Way

**Objective:** Show the client how outreach works in practice — and why the tone matters.

**Core message:** We lead with relevance, not a pitch. The goal of the first message is a conversation, not a conversion.

* LinkedIn connection requests are short, human, and free of sales language
* After connection, a brief follow-up message opens a conversation around a likely challenge
* Tone is consultative: “Are you thinking about…?” — not “I have a building for you”
* We earn the right to talk about The Base by first demonstrating we understand their situation

**Suggested visual:** *A mock LinkedIn message thread — before (generic, salesy) vs after (consultative, specific).*

### Slide 9: Step 5 — Sharing Something That Creates Value

**Objective:** Show what happens after engagement — and why the asset approach converts conversations into viewings.

**Core message:** When a contact responds, we provide a specific, relevant asset — not a brochure. The asset is matched to the contact’s role and likely concern.

* Accessibility one-pager — for companies citing commute or travel challenges
* Amenity overview — for HR and People contacts focused on employee experience
* Size-matched availability overview — for contacts at an active search stage
* Workplace comparison (The Base vs standard Amsterdam office) — for contacts evaluating the market
* Spot Schiphol Community overview — for contacts who value culture and business community

**Suggested visual:** *A small “asset library” grid — five content cards with title, icon, and target audience label.*

### Slide 10: From Signal to Conversation: A Typical Journey

**Objective:** Make the process tangible with a real-world example scenario.

**Core message:** The entire process — from intent signal to first viewing — can take 3–6 weeks, with low friction at every step.

* Week 1: LeadInfo identifies a 90-person IT company in Rotterdam visiting The Base’s Schiphol Real Estate page
* Week 2: DMU mapped — HR Director and Operations Manager identified on LinkedIn
* Week 3: Connection requests sent and accepted
* Week 4: Consultative follow-up sent to HR Director and Operations Manager
* Week 5: HR Director responds positively — Amenity overview and size-matched availability note shared
* Week 6: In-person viewing booked

**Suggested visual:** *A timeline graphic — six numbered weeks with icons and brief text at each stage.*

### Slide 11: How Many Companies Are We Talking About?

**Objective:** Help the client understand the scale of the opportunity.

**Core message:** There are hundreds of qualifying companies within commercial reach of The Base. The method helps us focus on the highest-probability targets.

* Estimated universe of qualifying companies (50–200 employees, target sectors, A4 corridor): 400–800 companies
* Monthly intent signal volume (estimated, tool-dependent): 15–40 new companies per month
* Monthly DMU outreach capacity (per FTE): 30–50 contacts per month
* Expected connection acceptance rate: 30–50%
* Expected follow-up response rate: 8–15% of those who connect
* Estimated monthly qualified conversations: 5–15 per month

**Suggested visual:** *A funnel graphic showing the conversion path from Total universe down to Qualified conversations.*

### Slide 12: Inputs and Enablers

**Objective:** Be transparent with the client about what is required on their side and the leasing team’s side.

**Core message:** This approach requires a modest set of tools, content, and team commitment — but delivers disproportionate output relative to passive marketing spend.

* Tools: DealFront or LeadInfo licence (approx. €500–1,200/month depending on package)
* LinkedIn: LinkedIn Sales Navigator (approx. €80–100/user/month)
* Content assets: 5–8 reusable, designed assets for The Base (one-time production cost)
* Team time: Approximately 4–6 hours per week per outreach FTE
* CRM: Tracking and pipeline management (HubSpot, Salesforce, or equivalent)
* Reporting cadence: Monthly pipeline review with the owner/client

**Suggested visual:** *A three-column Input → Process → Output graphic.*

**Core message:** Every step in the process can be measured. We will report on contacts made, conversations opened, viewings booked, and leads in pipeline — on a monthly basis.
